Design Principles

Design follows basic rules to ensure quality. Below you can find examples of a good use of whitespace, repetition, and contrast on mobile versions of websites.

Whitespace

Whitespace allows the user to rest their eyes and helps set the focus on the content provided. Apple uses this principle plenty on their website. It's main use on mobile is to make their content stand out and to emphasize the alignment of their elements. Mobile users benefit from this by only having a few things to look at in one time.

Repetition

Repetition is al over this site. Some of the repetitions are the font, the heading size, the spacing between paragraphs, and the line after each section. The repetition helps users understand how to interpret information and how it's related to other objects. W3schools uses this to show each block of rules using a larger heading size at the top of each new section.

Contrast

Contrast is meant to grab the user's attention and immediately get them to pay attention to a specific object. theFutur uses this rule as soon as you land on their site. Their main image that takes up the entire viewport and uses a monochromatic color palette to make the text stand out above all else. The contrast helps drive attention.
